Purpose of the Study

The goal of this project is to develop human-machine collaboration tools to provide end-users greater autonomy in shaping their social media content.

Study Procedures

In the first part of this study, we conducted interviews with video creators on sites like YouTube and Twitch. Based on our findings from these interviews, we have created a tool to help YouTube creators and moderators manage their comments more efficiently. Now, we are looking for participantes to test this tool and help us understand its utility and deficiencies. We are looking for YouTube creators who have at least a few hundred comments posted on their channel and who are familiar with the 'Blocked Words' setting on YouTube studio. Each interview will last less than 90 minutes and participants will be compensated $25 for their time.

During the interview, we will have the participant sign in to our tool using their YouTube account. Our tool will load the comments posted to the participant's channel. Then we will walk the participant through how our tool can help them moderate their comments. As the participant explores this tool, we would ask them to speak aloud how they would use this tool, what other features they would like to have, etc.

Risks, Stress or Discomfort

You may suffer emotional distress talking about the experiences of seeing offensive comments online. To avoid this, we encourage you to only relate these experiences to us if you can do so without distressing yourself.

Benefits of the Study

We will develop moderation tools based on our interviews with users like you. These tools may help you better moderate the comments you receive on your videos online.
